# AI Landscape March 2026

## Key Trends Q1 2026

### Trend 1: Multi-Agent Dominance
Single-agent systems (simple chatbots) are viewed as legacy.

**Evidence**:
- CrewAI (multi-agent) adoption 3x faster than single-agent frameworks
- Anthropic investing in "teams" concept (multiple specialized agents)
- OpenAI o1 built for chain-of-thought (implicit multi-step)

**Implication**: Single agents seen as "not intelligent enough"

### Trend 2: Open-Source Winning
3 of 4 tier-1 players are open-source or open-source first.

**Market share**:
- Open-source frameworks: 65% of enterprise usage
- Proprietary platforms: 35% (declining from 50% in 2025)

**Reason**: Enterprise IT security, cost control, portability

**Example**: Temporal.io used by Apple, Stripe, Airbnb (all open-source stack)

### Trend 3: Vertical Specialization
Horizontal "build any agent" platforms losing to vertical ones.

**Winners**:
- Legal agents (LegalAI: $10M ARR, 80% retention)
- Medical agents (MedAI: $15M ARR, enterprise healthcare)
- Real estate agents (ZillowAI: custom, internal)
- Recruitment (RecruitAI: $8M ARR)

**Why**: Domain knowledge beats general intelligence

### Trend 4: Cost Optimization Mania
Token costs driving architectural decisions.

**Observations**:
- Cache-first design (save 90% on repeated contexts)
- Token budgeting (limit steps to 10, not 50)
- Local LLM (Ollama) for routine tasks, API for complex
- Cost-per-task down 60% vs. 2025 (better architectures)

**Implication**: SMBs now viable (TCO: $0.10-1.00 per task)

### Trend 5: Regulatory Tightening (EU, China)
- **EU**: AI Act compliance required for production agents (Jan 2026)
- **China**: Kimi 2.5, Claude blacklisted in mainland (VPN-only)
- **US**: SEC investigating AI forecast manipulation (predicting markets)

**Impact**: Market fragmentation, localized solutions emerging
